a·gen·tic (ā-jen'tik)
Denotes self-directed actions aimed at personal development or personally chosen goals.
Other associations: dominance, qualities relevant for goal-attainment, such as assertiveness, competence or persistence, goal-achievement and task functioning.

Generally, words like leader, assertive, confident, intellectual, ambitious, dominant, forceful, independent and outspoken are known as “agentic” (active/assertive) words and have typically described men in the past (1).
Such imbalances may not be intentional; sometimes it is merely a lack of awareness. The amplification of these micro-inequities builds on existing stereotypes, and may seem innocuous.
